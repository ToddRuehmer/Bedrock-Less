Monthly Premiums

  • Monthly premiums – all states (except Puerto Rico & Guam)
  • Monthly premiums – Puerto Rico & Guam

Program highlights

MGIC's borrower-paid Monthly MI premiums are MGIC's most widely used option, serving the broadest audience.

When monthly premiums are cancelled, the monthly mortgage payment is reduced by the amount of the MI premium.

Coverage Coverage is provided through the date MI is cancelled.
Premium Due at Closing
  • Borrowers pay $0 at closing with MGIC’s Monthly option
Payment Terms
  • Borrowers pay monthly as part of their mortgage payment
  • MGIC bills lender for premium due
Cancellable? Coverage can be cancelled:
  • Based on investor requirements OR
  • Under the Homeowners Protection Act of 1998 (HPA)
Refundable?
  • A refundable option is available with a slight premium adjustment; refunds are prorated
  • If coverage is cancelled or terminated under the HPA, unearned premium is refunded

Annual Premiums

  • Annual premiums – all states (except Puerto Rico & Guam)
  • Annual premiums – Puerto Rico & Guam

Program highlights

When annual premiums are cancelled, the monthly mortgage payment is reduced by the amount of the MI premium.

Coverage Coverage is provided through the date MI is cancelled.
Premium Due at Closing

Borrowers pay first-year premium in full at closing.
Payment Terms
  • Borrowers pay 1/12th of the premium monthly as part of their mortgage payment
  • MGIC bills lender for premium due
Cancellable? Coverage can be cancelled:
  • Based on investor requirements OR
  • Under the Homeowners Protection Act of 1998 (HPA)
Refundable?
  • For loans insured under our refundable Annual Premium program with an initial insurance effective date prior to July 29, 1999, we will provide refunds based on our Annual Premium Short Rate Refund Schedule
  • For loans insured under our refundable Annual Premium program with an initial insurance effective date of July 29, 1999, and after, we will provide refunds on a prorated basis
    • The calculation is based on the number of days in force divided by 365 days

Single Premiums

  • Non-Refundable single premiums – all states (except WA)
  • Non-Refundable single premiums – WA
  • Refundable single premiums – all states (except WA)
  • Refundable single premiums – WA

Program highlights


Coverage Coverage is provided until scheduled amortization reaches 78% of original value, unless coverage has been previously cancelled, but coverage will continue for any existing default.
Premium Due at Closing A one-time, single payment at closing can be:
  • Paid by the borrowers or by a third party, such as a builder or a seller OR
  • Financed into the loan amount
Cancellable? Coverage can be cancelled:
  • Based on investor requirements OR
  • Under the Homeowners Protection Act of 1998 (HPA)
Refundable?
  • When cancelled under the HPA, refundable and non-refundable single premiums are refunded based on our HPA refund schedules
  • When refundable single premiums are cancelled, refunds are calculated on a pro rata basis over a 5-year term
  • See Refund Schedules

Split Premiums

  • Split premiums – all states

Program highlights

MGIC's Split Premiums give your borrowers the option of paying part of the MI premium up front in order to reduce the monthly MI premium paid along with their mortgage payment. Borrowers can choose the initial premium rate, which is a percentage of the loan amount.

Coverage Coverage is provided through the date MI is cancelled.
Premium Due at Closing The upfront portion of the premium due at closing can be:
  • Paid by the borrowers or by a third party, such as a builder or a seller OR
  • Financed into the loan amount
Payment Terms
  • Borrowers pay the remaining monthly premium as part of their mortgage payment
  • MGIC bills lender for premium due
Cancellable? Coverage can be cancelled:
  • Based on investor requirements OR
  • Under the Homeowners Protection Act of 1998 (HPA)
Refundable? Split premiums are non-refundable, unless coverage is cancelled or terminated under the HPA; then the refund is based on our HPA refund schedules

Monthly Premiums

  • For LPMI Monthly premiums, see the adjustments table on the Borrower-Paid Monthly premium card – all states (except Puerto Rico & Guam)
  • Monthly premiums – Puerto Rico & Guam

Program highlights

MGIC's lender-paid monthly premium program provides a cost-effective, efficient alternative to our standard borrower-paid MI premium rates.

Coverage Lender-Paid Monthly Premiums provide coverage while MI premiums are being paid.
Premium Due at Closing
  • No premium is due at closing
  • Billing is deferred until the first full month after closing; payment is due the following month and continues until the insurance is cancelled
  • The premium must be paid by the lender or anyone other than the borrowers and must not be charged separately to the borrowers
Refundable? LPMI Monthlies are non-refundable.

Single Premiums

  • Single premiums – all states (except Puerto Rico & Guam)
  • Single premiums – Puerto Rico
  • Single premiums – Guam

Program highlights

MGIC's lender-paid single premium program provides a cost-effective, efficient alternative to our standard borrower-paid MI premium rates.

The cost of the single premium may be recouped by building it into the loan interest rate or the origination fee. This provides your borrowers with additional tax deductibility because of the increased interest rate. Origination fees also may be paid by a third party, such as a builder or a seller.

Coverage Coverage is provided for the life of the loan.
Premium Due at Closing

A one-time, single payment at closing:

  • Must be paid by the lender or anyone other than the borrowers AND
  • Must not be charged separately to the borrowers
Refundable? LPMI Singles are non-refundable.
